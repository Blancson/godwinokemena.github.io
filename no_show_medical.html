About Project

Project Brief: TThe issue of patients not showing up for their medical appointments ("no-shows") presents a significant challenge for healthcare providers, leading to inefficiencies, increased costs, and disrupted schedules. This analysis aims to understand the factors contributing to no-show appointments and provide actionable insights to reduce their occurrence.

Project Objective: The primary goal of this analysis is to identify patterns and predictors of no-show appointments using historical appointment data. By understanding these factors, healthcare providers can develop strategies to improve appointment adherence and optimize scheduling.

Data Gathering: The dataset used for this analysis includes records of medical appointments, capturing various attributes such as:

  • Patient demographics (age, gender)
  • Appointment details (date, time, scheduled day)
  • Appointment status (show or no-show)
  • Other factors (SMS reminders, medical conditions)

    • Some of the data wrangling steps carried out during the analysis involves and not limited to change of dtype of some columns, creating a new column from previous one, and removing irrelevant columns as well as rows with missing entries.

    Key Metrics: The study analyzed the percentage of appointments where patients did not show up, analyzed demographics, temporal factors like appointment day, time, lead time, SMS reminders, appointment confirmation, and health and socioeconomic factors like medical conditions and insurance type. It also examined the impact of appointment confirmation calls or emails.

    Summary of Findings Patients aged 18-35 and males are more likely to miss appointments, with lower socioeconomic backgrounds and scheduling conflicts contributing to higher no-show rates. Appointments scheduled on Mondays and Fridays, during the day, and far in advance have higher no-show rates. SMS reminders and confirmation calls have shown effectiveness in reducing no-show rates. Chronic conditions and public insurance type also contribute to higher no-show rates.

    Conclusions: The analysis identifies several key factors influencing no-show rates for medical appointments. By addressing these factors, healthcare providers can implement targeted strategies to improve appointment adherence, optimize scheduling, and reduce the negative impact of no-shows.
