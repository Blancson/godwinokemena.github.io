About Project

Project Brief: Maven Coffee Shop is looking to break into the US coffee market. They need insights from “The Great American Coffee Taste Test” to open their first coffee shop. The report involves analyzing data to make data-driven decisions on:

  • Target Audience
  • Product Offering
  • Pricing Strategy

    • Key Insights: The dataset for the project was provided by Mavens Analytics and can be found here. This project was done entirely using Microsoft PowerBI. For most visuals, questionnaires with no response were filtered out.

    Target Audience: A total of 4,042 respondents took part in this survey with approximately 62.4% of the respondents as Male and 21.1% as Female.

    The target audience for the coffee shop is quite diverse, but the millennials (5-34 years) have been identified as the primary demographic, with a significant portion of coffee consumption attributed to them (49.13%).

    Among the surveyed respondents, 43.5% are bachelor’s degree holders followed by master’s degree holder with 18.3%. Most respondents are Employed, mainly Working from home.

    Majority (77%) respondents identified as Whites, 12% as Asians and 6% identified as Hispanic. 75% of the respondents are without children and 11.8% have 2 children.

    Product Preference:

    For majority of the respondents, their Favourite type of beverage was Pourover. Regarding coffee preferences,

    Full Caffeine coffee dominated, with flavours such as Fruity and Chocolatey being preferred with Light roast level.

    Respondents mainly consume Black coffee, without additives, occasionally with milk or diary. Majority respondents enjoy the taste of coffee. Most respondents prefer their coffee Somewhat Strong.

    From the taste sample given, Coffee D was preferred more by 36.7%. Surprisingly, most respondents are interested on where their coffee comes from.

    Pricing Strategy:.

    The age category 25-44 years spends between $20 - $60 on coffee monthly, a 50% of monthly coffee expenses. This category also accounts for 75% of coffee consumers. From the first matrix table, we can notice as the age increases, the monthly expense on coffee reduces. Coffee drinking is more common with younger people

    The optimum amount people are willing to pay for a cup of coffee is $8 - $10. 40% of the respondents are willing to buy a cup of coffee at this price and 30% of the respondents is willing to pay more for premium quality. 58.5% agree to get value for money spent on a cup of coffee while 41.5% think otherwise.

    My Recommendations

    Target Audience: The target audience are age group of 25-44 years since they take one cup or more of coffee daily and are willing to spend more for a cup of coffee compared to other age group.

    Product Offering: Lightly roast, Pourover is the most preferred drink. Natural Coffee D should not be offered as the main option due to some dislikes but can be an option on the menu. Taste is the main reason for taking coffee, thus this quality must be preserved in the products.

    Pricing Strategy: Offer regular Coffee between $6-10. There is also a potential market to offer high-value and specialty Coffee between $10-$15 and premium coffee to a targeted market over $20.
